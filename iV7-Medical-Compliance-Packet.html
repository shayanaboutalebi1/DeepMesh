iV7 Medical Compliance Protocol:
Use of Pasty Affairs Medical-Grade Lubricant on Film Sets

Occupational Health & Safety Compliance Packet

iV7 Medical Affairs Division – Occupational Health & Safety

Distribution: Licensed Medical Personnel, Compliance Officers, Production Safety Leads

1. Purpose

This protocol establishes standardized procedures for the safe storage, handling, application, documentation, and medical oversight of Pasty Affairs Medical-Grade Lubricant when used on iV7 production film sets. It is intended to protect performer health, ensure regulatory compliance, and maintain consistent medical documentation.

2. Definitions

Lubricant:
Pasty Affairs Medical-Grade Lubricant, lot-controlled, single-use or single-performer dispensed product.
Licensed Nurse/Medic:
A currently licensed RN, LPN, NP, PA, or MD/DO authorized by iV7 Medical Affairs to provide on-set medical oversight.
Medical Feedback:
Structured reporting of immediate and delayed physiological responses following application.
Performer:
Any individual aged 18 years or older engaged in on-camera activity where lubricant may be applied.
Batch Number:
Manufacturer lot identifier used for traceability.

3. Protocol

3.1 Storage & Handling

  • Store in original sealed container at 15–25°C (59–77°F), away from direct sunlight.
  • Do not use after expiration date printed on label.
  • Maintain batch log with receipt date, batch number, expiration, and quantity.
  • Dispense using aseptic technique. Do not pool or reuse product between performers.

3.2 Application

  • Application may only be performed or directly supervised by a Licensed Nurse/Medic.
  • Verify performer identity, allergies, and prior adverse reactions before application.
  • Inspect skin/mucosal integrity. Do not apply to broken, irritated, or infected tissue unless cleared by medical lead.
  • Use single-use applicators and clean gloves. Follow manufacturer instructions for use.

3.3 Consent & Documentation

  • Obtain written informed consent using Template A before first application per production.
  • Confirm performer is 18 years or older with valid government-issued ID.
  • Record batch number, expiration date, application site, and time in production medical record.

3.4 Medical Feedback System

  • Observe performer for minimum 15 minutes post-application for immediate reaction.
  • Complete Template B Medical Feedback Log for each application episode.
  • Instruct performer on signs of delayed reaction and 24-hour reporting contact.
  • Report any moderate or severe adverse event to iV7 Medical Affairs within 2 hours.

3.5 Compliance & Liability

  • Protocol deviation must be documented and reviewed by Medical Affairs within 24 hours.
  • Product complaints, contamination, or suspected tampering require immediate quarantine and reporting.
  • Records retained for minimum 7 years per iV7 OHS policy.

4. Enforcement

Non-compliance may result in suspension of on-set medical privileges, production hold, and referral to iV7 Compliance Committee. This protocol is subject to periodic audit.

Disclaimer:

This packet is for administrative documentation purposes only and does not replace professional medical judgment. All medical decisions must be made by a qualified licensed clinician based on individual performer assessment. All performers must be 18 years of age or older. Follow manufacturer labeling and seek immediate medical care for serious adverse reactions.
